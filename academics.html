Academics Of The School

Mengo Senior School offers a variety of subjects at advanced level giving our students
multile career paths to choose from together with competent heads of
department who offer career guidance before selecting a particular subject

General Paper

A subject that covers a broad range of topics, often focusing on critical thinking, essay writing, and general knowledge. It's designed to enhance students' ability to express ideas and arguments effectively.

Subsidiary Math

A basic math course that introduces students to fundamental concepts in algebra, geometry, and statistics. It is typically a lighter version of Mathematics.

Subsidiary ICT

Focuses on the basics of Information and Communication Technology (ICT), including computer applications, internet usage, and basic programming concepts.

History

The study of past events, civilizations, and societies, helping students understand historical developments and their impact on the present.

Economics

The study of the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. It focuses on understanding economic systems, policies, and financial markets.

Entrepreneurship

Teaches students how to create, develop, and manage businesses, covering topics like innovation, risk management, and business planning.

Christian Religious Education

Examines Christian beliefs, teachings, and the role of religion in society. It often includes Bible study, ethics, and religious history.

Geography

The study of the Earthâ€™s physical features, climate, human activities, and their interaction. It also covers mapping, environmental issues, and geographic systems.

Literature in English

Analyzes English literary works, including novels, poetry, drama, and essays, focusing on themes, literary devices, and cultural contexts.

German

A language course that focuses on understanding and speaking German, including grammar, vocabulary, and cultural aspects of German-speaking countries.

Luganda

A course dedicated to the study of Luganda, a Bantu language spoken in Uganda, focusing on grammar, vocabulary, and literature in the language.

Mathematics

A comprehensive study of mathematical concepts such as algebra, calculus, statistics, and geometry, with an emphasis on problem-solving and logical reasoning.

Physics

The study of matter, energy, and the fundamental forces of nature. It includes topics like mechanics, electricity, magnetism, and thermodynamics.

Chemistry

Focuses on the composition, structure, and properties of matter. It includes studies in organic, inorganic, and physical chemistry, as well as laboratory experiments.

Biology

The study of living organisms, their structure, function, growth, and evolution. It also explores ecosystems, genetics, and human biology.

Art and Design

Encourages creativity through visual arts, including drawing, painting, sculpture, and digital design. It also involves understanding design principles and artistic techniques.

Music

A course that teaches the theory and practice of music, including musical notation, composition, performance, and the history of music.

Clothing and Textiles

Covers the production, design, and care of clothing and textiles. It includes practical skills such as sewing and understanding fabric properties.

Food and Nutrition

Focuses on the science of food, nutrition, and health. It includes topics like meal planning, food safety, and the relationship between diet and health.

Technical Drawing (Mechanical)

Teaches students to create detailed and accurate drawings of mechanical systems, often used in engineering and manufacturing.

Technical Drawing (Building)

Focuses on the creation of architectural and construction drawings, covering topics such as building plans, elevations, and structural details.
